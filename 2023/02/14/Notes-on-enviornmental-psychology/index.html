Environmental Psychology: An Introduction

By Linda Steg and Judith I. M. De Groot

    • Environmental stress: e.g., noise, crowding
    • Visual landscape assessment & perception
    • Health benefits of nature
    • Restorative environments: 1) stress recovery theory 2) attention restoration theory; 1) perceptual fluency account 2) contentedness to nature 3) micro-restorative experiences and instorative effects
    • Ambivalence towards nature and natural landscape
    • Wildlife: 1) the cognitive hierarchy, 2) wildlife value orientations, 3) norms & attitudes towards wildlife, 4) Emotions towards wildlife.
    • Built environments/building. Urban environmental quality
    • Environment and quality of life.
  • Environmental behavior
    • Environmental cues
      • Goal framing theory: 1) normative goal, 2) Gain goal, 3) Hedonic goal
      • Environmental cues ↔ normative goals
    • Measuring environmental behavior
    • Values: 1) social value orientations, 2) Schwartz’s value theory, 3) Emergence of biospheric values

    • Social dilemmas
      • Types: large-scale dilemmas; resources dilemmas; public good dilemmas
      • Motives: greed, efficiency, fairness
      • Factors -> cooperation: group size, communication, response efficacy, environmental uncertainty, social uncertainty, norms in large-scale dilemma.
    • Explaining environmental behavior: TPB, NAM, VBN theory of environmentalism, Goal Framing Theory.

      • Rewards & penalties
      • Persuasive strategies
      • Acceptability of environmental policies
      • Behavioral change
